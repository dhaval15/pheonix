Uncertainity, Chaos and God

While watching a movie, I saw a scientist who prayed to God for the success of the project they were working on. Now I’m not saying that it would be illogical for an scientist to believe in God. At many times, I have prayed to some entity that I am unable to define yet.

The question is however, if given the chance to that scientist or me to believe in God, in perfectly normal situation. Scientist might have thought it a waste of time to discuss on existance of God. Same with me, I rather have time to waste, so I would have outrightly rejected the existance of the God and would have indulged in a debate with believers in God.

However, that changes when situation changes, both the scientist and me wants an understanding of whatever happening around us. Failure, on perfectly planned project is hard to concieve as an understandable notion and hence we can’t be rely on science to be big brother and make us understand why the hell it failed. So we are left with the uncontrollables, that we want to understand but at the time, we couldn’t. And hence, we introduce a new entity that is higher than the science, this doesn’t have to be God, it could be anything, a mosquito who is bothering me at the laptop screen can be it, anything. For that scientist, it’s God, for me, well it’s still an entity indefinable.

The entity is rather illusion, illusion of control. Control where control is not possible. And what if this illusion of control have thier rules, constitution, then it’s a religion. But what’s at the center of religion, it’s always a God. Because what we need is not religion but a god, an illusion of control.

Now every individual have their own imagination for their entity or God, while this helped to believe that they were in the control. However with each individual came a different imagination and a different God. Different representation between sevearal individuals might not be productive when they had to work together for living, so at some point of time each illusion of control merged into a larger and general to population, illusion of control. Still the perception by each individual were the same.

This served both the purpose as collective of individual, a population had illusion of control that was symmetric to all and the individual illusion of control still preserved.

In a sense, people who believe in God, has two Gods, their common God which they share with other people in the population and their own God which they deny the existence, but exist in their mind as an illusion of control. People who don’t believe in God, are those who are content with their individual God/entity.

So as an outcome of evolution in religion, Christianity and Islam chose the monoethist and Hinduism remained polyethiest but at the individual level, all of them still remains polyethiest having as number of Gods as the population.
