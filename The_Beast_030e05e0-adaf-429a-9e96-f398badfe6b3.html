The Beast

I grasped my breath again and locked the door, the constant thudd on the door was immitating in me as a wave of fear. What I wanted to do was unmemorize those yellow glowing eyes, instead they started multiplicating and as my fear multiplicated and at that moment I felt I was more scared when the beast was behind the door, like anytime I can see his claws ripping the door apart and then doing the same to my body. But then a few seconds later I gathered myself, I realized I've to think quick, but the slate against my brain was clear and white. I picked up the phone, and skimmed through the contacts, my brother, my father, whom would I call, nobody. Then a number which was not on the contact list, 100, but that was too risky, I would prefer myself eaten alive in a single day over a prison. I discarded the thought.

A powerful blow vibrated the door as much that I thought it was almost striped from its hinges. And as if now the beast found its voice, it was growling heavily, I suspected why didn't it howl earlier, but the next thought didn't give space to earlier one. What about my girls?

I skipped the steps while climbing up stairs and almost flipped upside down. I managed to clutch the wooden railing and climbed up the stairs in a single breath. Behind me I closed the door shut and checked it three times. I knew it was closed and I even knew that the beast was getting stronger and stronger, and would be on the other side of the door, growling, taking heavy breaths, with its glowing yellow eyes. The thought of looking at the beast's yellow glittering eyes made me feel dizzy and with that state of dizziness, I lingered to the bedroom, where the girls were sleeping. How in the heaven, they were sleeping so quitely. I picked both of them and marched towards the main gate, but when I opened the door and looked outside, the blackness of the long forest that awaited, flipped my stomach upside down and again I was seeing a set of yellow glittering eyes, and I would have fell on the door itself, if it wasn't for my youngest one, who woke up to all this comotion.

"Where are we going, daddy?" She asked.

This was getting out of my control, with the beast thumping on the door heavily, my little one waking up and my brain out of ideas. The noise of the growl was becoming louder and louder, which now made my younger one cry and then it woke up the older one. At that point, I resented all the choices I made in my whole life. My whole life came before me like a graded exam paper where in multiple choice question I was given zero. Why did I left the pharmacutical company in the first place?, Why did I left my wife?, Why even I brought these two kids into the middle of nowhere or to this earth? And a most important question which shoot my brain in burstful thinking.

The girls were crying heavily and trying to clutch away from me. When I looked in their eyes, it seemed to me, if I hadn't held them in my strong arms, they would have been running through the forest without a flicker of fear popping in thier eyes. At that time, I got the answer, I would finish what I started.

I marched back to the bedroom, with the girls in my hand and laid them on the bed. "Daddy wouldn't let happen anything to you" I didn't even stopped to listen to their response, or the cryings which grew more afterwards. I couldn't, I knew if I stopped there then I wouldn't be able to stop the beast. I ran back to the door which lead to the basement, I could hear the screaming growls in the background of two set of crying shrills. I ran through the stairs and reached at the table where phone was lying idly. I didn't even dare to look at the door, where the beast had probably scrached its claws.

I picked up the dial, and until it was connected, I allowed myself to bear the pain of listening to low sobbing that pulsated my heart. Finnaly the call got connected.

"Mithilesh, come fast, call the police, call anyone you could" I said and then sighed. "Take good care of the girls."

"Okay, but what?" A manly voice said.

"We don't have time for that" I said and threw the dial back on the rest. Now I gathered the courage to look at the door where the beast was thumping heavily and fortunately there was only dent but no sign of tear. I turned right under the stairs, where three iron safe were laid, I pressed the code for the last one and the next second, it was open, and there laid a pistol and a special kind of knife, which rotated itself when pressed on the handle like a blade in a blender.

I took both out and sat on the sofa, and tried to force myself into a zen mode, where I could hear the breath of the beast, I could see the movement of each pawn of the beast, the teeth scritching against each other, the raised burrow and its ears. And its eyes and its yellow glittering eyes, they were terror and I couldn't look beyond those. I couldn't hear the thuds, the growl. It seemed I was in the space looking at the blackhole. And instead of absorbing all the light, it was emmiting bright yellow light and was slowly pulling my concious towards it, I was trying to look away but the very next moment I was looking back at the yellow glittering blackhole. I was starting to resent myself, if I hadn't had started already, there were so many questions that lingered in my concious, that I'd lost count of it.

And as I was pulled towards the yellow glittering lights, I felt that there was a black circle inside the yellow ring, and it was getting larger and larger. The glow started disappearing and now I was looking at the eternal blackness, I opened my eyes and read the sign on the board for the last time. DO NOT ENTER, it said.

I pointed the pistol at the door, and emptied all the pistol hoping it would be burried in either beast's head or its heart. I didn't care for weather my hope availed or not. I threw my pistol away and tightened my grip on the knife.

I turned the door open and against my eyes the beast struggling to get on its feet. I wound the door behind me, and there looking into the beast's big eyes I saw a reflection of myself, not very unlike the beast itself. In its eyes I found the answer for my last question, I created the beast cause' I was the beast.

And with feeling of fulfillment I raged towards the beast.
